304003. The Disintegration of the Timurid Empire

Khorasan — Not random

Triggered by

Action A of 296607 - Ascendancy of Ûlûgh Beg for Timurid Empire

Description

Upon the ascendancy of Ûlûgh Beg, the Empire split into many various warring factions. Most notably were the domains of the Princes of Samarkand, in the east, and those of the Kings of Khorasan, in the west. For the first few decades, whilst various Timurid princes competed for its throne, Khorasan would be wrought by conflict.

Actions

A. We shall be unstoppable!

  • Set flag [timkho] for events
  • +10000 infantry in the capital province
  • +5000 cavalry in a random province
  • +5000 cavalry in a random province
  • Start a war with Timurid Empire
  • Start a war with Fars
  • -400 relations with Timurid Empire
  • -400 relations with Fars
  • -100 relations with Uzbeks
  • Stability -4
  • Global revolt risk +5 for 36 months
  • Samarkand will be considered a claim province
  • uighur will no longer be an accepted culture
  • turkoman will no longer be an accepted culture
  • uighur will become an accepted culture
  • turkoman will become an accepted culture
  • Event 296603 - The Disintegration of the Timurid Empire for Timurid Empire is triggered immediately

304000. Kings of Khorasan

Khorasan — Not random

Conditions

  • Timurid Empire exists
  • The following must not occur:
    • Flag [timkho] is set

Will happen within 30 days of March 2, 1447
Checked again every 30 days until trigger is met (cannot happen after March 2, 1500)

Description

Founded by the Mongol conquerer Timur, the Timurid Empire after his death was never truly stable due to constant infighting. Eventually, many of the Timurid princes would polarize, developing two distinct power bases: one in Herat and the other in Samarkand.

Actions

A. Claim the right to the Timurid throne

  • uighur will no longer be an accepted culture
  • turkoman will no longer be an accepted culture
  • uighur will become an accepted culture
  • turkoman will become an accepted culture
  • +10000 infantry in the capital province
  • +5000 cavalry in the capital province
  • Break vassalization with Timurid Empire
  • Bukhara will be considered a national province
  • Samarkand will be considered a national province
  • Mekran will be considered a national province
  • Surkhandarya will be considered a national province
  • A random province revolts
  • A random province revolts
  • Stability -3
  • +100
  • -400 relations with Timurid Empire
  • -400 relations with Afghanistan
  • -400 relations with Uzbeks
  • Move capital to Herat
  • +50 victory points

B. Rescind all designs on the Timurid throne

  • Stability +1
  • uighur will no longer be an accepted culture
  • turkoman will no longer be an accepted culture
  • uighur will become an accepted culture
  • turkoman will become an accepted culture
  • +50 relations with Timurid Empire
  • +50 relations with Afghanistan
  • -400 relations with Uzbeks
  • -50 victory points

304001. Reclamation of the Timurid Empire

Khorasan — Not random

Conditions

  • The following must not occur:
    • Timurid Empire exists
  • Country is not at war
  • Own Samarkand
  • Control Samarkand

Will happen within 30 days of March 2, 1447
Checked again every 30 days until trigger is met (cannot happen after March 2, 1500)

Description

Upon reclaiming the capitals of the Timurid Empire, a Timurid prince could declare himself the ruler of the new Timurid Empire. With this declaration, however, came great responsibility.

Actions

A. Re-establish the Timurid Empire!

  • Flag graphics set to default
  • Become Timurid Empire
  • +10000 infantry in the capital province
  • +5000 cavalry in the capital province
  • Global revolt risk +3 for 420 months
  • Move capital to Samarkand

Khorasan — Not random

Triggered by
